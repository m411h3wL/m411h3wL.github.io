About Me

I'm a Bicomputational Engineering student at Lehigh University, which is a roundabout way of saying I split my time between biology, medicine, and code. Most of my research has been on the engineering side of healthcare access — I lead a project at Lehigh's Health Research Hub developing sustainable, low-cost biosensing electrodes, work I presented at the 2025 Biomedical Engineering Society conference in San Diego.

I try not to stay only on the research side of medicine. I trained as an EMT with St. Luke's University Health Network, spent a stretch as a clinical engineering design intern at Good Shepherd Rehabilitation Hospital, and ran a capstone with BBraun Medical redesigning a piece of clinical hardware. Seeing the patient side and the device side at the same time changes what you think is worth building.

Outside of that, I write small software tools for problems I actually have — mostly around automating my own job search. Lately I've been reading and thinking a lot about brain-computer interfaces, which is what most of my blog is about right now.
