About Me

I studied in Lehigh University's IDEAS Honors Program, an interdisciplinary program that combines engineering, arts, and sciences. Early in college, I had a pivotal conversation with an advisor about designing my education around a single question: how can we better understand the human condition?

That question shaped much of what followed.

I became interested not only in biology and computation, but also in decision-making, creativity, performance, health, and the systems that influence how people think, learn, and act. My academic work led me toward bioengineering research, machine learning applications in biology, and broader questions about how technology can augment human capability.

Today, I am particularly interested in neuroscience, brain-computer interfaces, human performance, and the evolving relationship between humans and technology. More broadly, I am fascinated by how ambitious people learn, create, collaborate, and make decisions under uncertainty.

Outside of science, I have a longstanding love for theatre and storytelling. I believe many of the same questions that drive science—what motivates people, how we make meaning, how we connect with one another—are explored through art as well. I hope to remain close to both worlds throughout my career.

I also grew up watching my mother transition from practicing law into a leadership role in marketing and communications. Learning about her work has given me a deep appreciation for storytelling, media, and the internet's ability to connect people with ideas. It reinforced my belief that publishing thoughtful work online is one of the most powerful ways to build relationships, share knowledge, and create opportunities.

This blog is my public notebook. It's a place to explore ideas, document what I'm learning, and think through questions at the intersection of science, technology, medicine, performance, and human potential. My hope is that anyone who visits will leave with a clear sense of the questions I'm pursuing and the kind of work I hope to contribute to in the years ahead.
