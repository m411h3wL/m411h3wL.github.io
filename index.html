On the Emulation of Minds

Brain Emulation: A Roadmap for the Ambitious & the Patient

The question is no longer whether the brain may one day be reproduced in silicon, but rather which obstacles shall fall first. The roadmap is long and littered with hard problems — connectome mapping, synaptic fidelity, energy budgets of frightful scale — and yet progress marches on at a pace that would unsettle prior generations.

This correspondent finds himself absorbed in the technical literature, tracing the distance between where neuroscience stands today and what a true whole-brain emulation would demand. The gap is vast. The momentum is real.

On the Profession of the Law

Public Relations for Lawyers: Reputation in an Age of Noise

The attorney who ignores his public image does so at his peril. In an era of instantaneous opinion and digital permanence, the lawyer's reputation is no longer built solely in courtrooms and club lounges — it is built, or destroyed, in full view of the searching public.

The study of public relations as applied to legal practice proves a curious one: equal parts old-fashioned trust-building and modern signal management. The principles, it turns out, are not so different from any other enterprise where credibility is the product.

— On the Automation of Commerce —

The generation of sales leads has long been a labor of telephone directories, cold calls, and considerable human patience. Today, the machines offer a different bargain. Automated prospecting, enrichment pipelines, and AI-qualified outreach now compress what once required a team of representatives into something closer to a single well-configured system. This writer is presently studying the architecture of such pipelines with an eye toward building them — not merely understanding them.

On Learning Itself

Teaching the Machine to Teach

Automation of learning — the construction of systems that acquire knowledge, refine it, and act upon it without constant human shepherding — may prove to be the most consequential subject of this decade. The writer approaches it with appropriate seriousness.
