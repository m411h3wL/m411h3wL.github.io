The Future of the SME

SME. Subject Matter Expert. A beautifully important acronym of our time.

June 29, 2026 3 min read

I’ve read a lot this week about bottlenecks to AI Adoption.

Here is my predicted order of operations:

First

Most humans continue to fumble around with sycophantic frontier models.

Some humans discover ways to reduce sycophancy; in turn they improve their own semantic and procedural knowledge.

They learn prompts such as “Show me where my mental model breaks down?” and “What is the bottleneck preventing me from reaching expert performance?”

These humans understand the Zone of Proximal Development.

They attempt to reduce the friction of learning through methods closely associated with their academic studies: strengthening associations through repetition, building projects, maybe even building personalized data pipelines to improve retrieval augmentation.

Eventually they come to understand the future viability of becoming a SME like this is futile. It will work for the next couple of years, at least.

But they worry about the window of opportunity in knowledge work.

At least we can say, if they’ve iterated and improved on this method for about a year, they’ve grown their knowledge base at a scale unimaginable to the American college system.

They can contribute their knowledge. They might even be considered true SMEs. But not at the level of the other humans who have adopted this same methodology last year with an even greater knowledge base.

The most knowledgeable people get significantly smarter. And now the baseline for knowledge to enter a profession has shot into the sky.

But at least maybe you’ve got your foot in the door. I might still do this with a scientific domain I am interested in. Maybe it’s naïve to think I could understand all of cancer biology in a year, but somehow, I think with this technology and consistency I very well could.

Regardless…

Second

CEOs, Founders, Majority Stakeholders of corporations learn these two facts:

  • Generally intelligent pretrained models already exist availably to the public.
  • Methods of fine-tuning and RLing generally intelligent models into identical versions of your top knowledge worker will be readily available, at a reasonable cost.

However, you’ll need to convince your V.P. of Accounting, John through a reasonable severance package, that you’ll need access to all his digital data and wetware for a couple of days, a week tops.

And you’ll download John, transforming the generally intelligent boilerplate into John.

John was exceptionally creative and astute at asking the right questions to improve his utility. But the trainer you paid to download John’s brain already knows this. So, they downloaded the part of John’s mental processes that iterates on his ability to improve his utility.

Now, every day, John talks to you through email, and maybe even on a future model of Alibaba’s Wan Streamer, a video model.

Third

Humans realize adoption just meant replacement all along. I’m not an economist, yet ha-ha, kind of not joking here… so I won’t pretend to say something like this causes a widening socioeconomic divide between the proletariat and the bourgeoisie… but I will say it really freakin’ sucks for anyone who doesn’t outright own means of production.

In conclusion, SME is an important acronym. Learn it. I have lots of thoughts on how to improve this dilemma but this is the current headspace I find myself at. I think we have time to make incorrect steps, for now.
