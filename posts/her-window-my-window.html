Her Window, My Window

A work in progress on what's actually different between two people's machines, even when it's the same model on both ends.

June 20, 2026 3 min read · in progress

This one's still being written — for now, just the opening.

I was attempting to help install software tools linked to ChatGPT for my mom the other day. A simple google drive add-on in order to prompt the auto-population of google sheets. It had taken me no time at all to do this on my own ChatGPT window. But as I sat down to explain this problem to her window, I found myself extremely frustrated that it didn’t understand my queries as clearly as my window had. So I tried to reframe it as if I were describing the installation and usage to my mom instead of to my personal intelligent assistant. And eventually I got there. Prodding, shaping the system, as Littau writes.

I taught my mom the workflow required to prompt GPT to manipulate a google sheet – go into google sheets yourself, create a sheet, copy the link, go to the + sign by the chat box, go to more, and add the drive app to the current chat. Then input the google sheets link, and tell it what you want. I want x ___ so that x. This language, although simple, is powerful in prompting.

I have my own theory - deviating from this formula, or a close relative to it, i.e. mindlessly prompting, promotes a relationship with your “chatbot/ai/agent” whatever you want to call it, that will spin you in loops and assume you are a bumbling idiot who doesn’t know what they want. So yes, prod and shape the system, but be as intentional as possible.

It comes from a project management SCRUM system. After some shallow digging I assume it comes from this page from Dan North.
